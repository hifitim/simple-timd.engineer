2017/04/10

Finished the Brothers Karamozov. It got a little dry toward the end, with the defense and prosecution droning on a bit in a fit of "philosophizing".

Now I've started The Shape of the Liturgy, by Dom Gregory Dix. The previous owner, one John Richardson of Rice University, apparently only got to page 14, because his multitudinous underlining and margin-note-taking stops abruptly at that point. I've taken it over and will continue his underlining and note-taking for him, hopefully to completion.

The garden, having been planted in late February and taken diligent care since then, is now producing fruit! We harvested several excellent cucumbers already, with a handful more to come in the next week or so, to judge by their size. There are several small Homestead and Better Boy tomatoes sprouting as well, which we hope the birds won't eat before we can pick them. The tomatillo plant, which has had a number of bright yellow flowers for several weeks but produced no fruit, needs to be cross-pollinated. Of course, when we bought it, we did buy a second. The girl at the checkout, however, broke it. So it is growing, but I don't think that it will be producing any flowers soon enough for us to use to pollinate the bigger plant. Live and learn, I guess.

Finally, I spent my "points" that my manager gave me at work for some things that I did, and I ordered a new Yamaha R-S500 stereo. It'll match my S-300 CD player much better than my older (but admittedly still totally functional) RX-797 that I bought second-hand in college.

Books to Read

2017/02/18

The Karamazov Brothers

About 1/3 of the way through. Not as bad as I thought it might be. I like this Oxford World's Classic translation. I tried the P&V, and it wasn't as good as I thought it might be. Monks punctuating their theological arguments with "So be it!" instead of "Amen"? "Dmitry Karamazov doesn't exist yet"? No thanks.
Also, the pages are away too thin, unfortunately - so the pages have to be kept close together until you're absolutely ready to turn.

Dialogue Concerning Heresies
